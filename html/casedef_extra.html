ILI case definitions

The following case definition for influenza-like illness is recommended by the ECDC:

ILIecdc - ECDC recommendation
  • Sudden onset of symptoms
  • Fever or feverishness (chills) or Malaise or Headache or Myalgia (muscle pain))
  • Cough or Sore throat or Shortness of breath

From season 2011-2012 on, all countries participating in Influenanet will use the same questionnaire and will apply this case definition. Many national surveillance systems do not apply the ECDC recommendation, but rather apply an ILI case definition which requires fever, especially to distinguish between a real influenza infection and a common cold. We therefore introduce a variation of the ILIecdc definition which does require fever:

ILIfever - Requiring fever
  • Sudden onset of symptoms
  • Fever or feverishness (chills)
  • Malaise or Headache or Myalgia (muscle pain))
  • Cough or Sore throat or Shortness of breath

Within the Influenzanet system, multiple ILI case definitions can be used simultaneously. ILIecdc has a higher sensitivity, since more participants with influenza will fit the definition. ILIecdc has a higher specificity, since fewer participants who do not have influenza, will fit the definition.

For historic seasons, both ILIecdc and ILIfever definitions cannot be applied. First, malaise and shortness of breath were mostly not reported in the first seasons of influenzanet. More importantly, until 2010 participants did not report the sudden onset of symptoms, but only the sudden onset of the fever if they had a measured temperature over 38 degrees Celsius. Therefore, we introduce the following case definition:

ILIhist - Seasons before 2010
  • Measured temperature ≥ 38 degrees Celsius
  • (Sudden onset of symptoms) or (Sudden onset of fever)
  • (Headache) or (Myalgia)
  • (Cough) or (Sore throat)

The ILIhist can be equally applied to most countries and seasons. However, due to cultural differences between countries participants could have different customs when to measure their temperature in case of a (suspected) fever, or could estimate their temperature.

38C any PT - 38C 38C or fever any IT - 38C any UK - any any

By requiring fever (measured, non-measured or shivers), the ILIe case definition is transformed to:

ILIf - Fever required
  • (Fever) or (Cold shivers) or (Measured temperature ≥ 38 degrees Celsius)
  • (Sudden onset of symptoms) or (Sudden onset of fever)
  • (Headache) or (Myalgia)
  • (Cough) or (Sore throat)

Previous scientific publications on Influenzanet data, usually required myalgia (muscle pain) as an essential element in the case definition:

ILIm - Myalgia and temperature ≥38 Celsius required
  • Measured temperature ≥ 38 degrees Celsius
  • (Sudden onset of symptoms) or (Sudden onset of fever)
  • (Myalgia)
  • (Cough) or (Sore throat)

This case definition seem to give the most consistent results in comparison with the ILI incidence as measured by medical doctors.
